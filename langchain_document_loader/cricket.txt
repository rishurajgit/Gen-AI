The bat sang loudly to the dancing breeze,
A moonlit six flew over whispering trees.
The stumps stood proud like ancient kings,
While leather dreams grew silver wings.
Each over painted stories in the sky,
Crowds roared like thunder passing by.
The pitch became a poet's golden page,
Where heroes battled time, not age.
When the final wicket kissed the ground,
Cricket left its heartbeat in every sound.
